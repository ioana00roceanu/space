Water importance and recycling Water is arguably the most important biochemical compound. All living organisms have a basic need for water in order to function. It is important for many reasons, such as:
• It is a major component of cells (about 85% of cells is water, in average); humans are 60% water;
• It regulates body temperature through transpiration;
• It serves as a lubricant in digestion and almost all other bodily processes; • It is an essential solvent for biochemical reactions to take place in;

Atmosphere

Atmosphere

For human life to be carried out on a space station, the existence of an atmosphere is mandatory, as is the presence of 760 mmHg pressure and the availability of water. The air composition aboard Fibonacci will strongly resemble the composition of Earth's atmospheric air. Excess carbon dioxide resulting from human breath will be channeled to the atmosphere in the agricultural torus, and in return some of the oxygen resulted from plant respiration will be transported to the human destined torus.
