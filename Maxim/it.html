Project Fibonacci

IT System

By placing Fibonacci in a sun-synchronous orbit around mars, we ensure that at no time will Mars be between the colony and earth, thus maximizing the period of time in which the colony and earth have the possibility of communicating. The system used for communications will be a deep-space lasercom system, using lasers to encode data in single photons. With current technology, such a system would be able to transport information at a rate of 100MB/s to and from Mars, but undergoing research should ensure that by 2020 we will have lasercoms capable of transporting information at rates of 1 GB/s over the same distance.


1) Infrastructure
All the information will be stored using 15 PB worth of storing capacity and backed-up using another 15PB. The total 30PB worth of storing capacity will materialize in about 35000 (including spares) hard drives with capacity of 1 TB. These hard-drives will be split and located in two distinct locations inside the Home Torus, for reasons related to data safety. We chose to put the hard drives inside the Home Torus, as it is the most radiation shielded.


2) User interface
The users will have access to the stored information through a small computer in their households. Users will also have the privilege of ordering for download files from Earth that are not on the local hard-drives. They will be able to do that in the limit of no more than 200 GB per month. Also, everything downloaded will have to be stored in the limit of what personal storage space they have left (see 2.2.4)
